# CUL Programmering — Intent

## Why We Exist

CUL Programmering is a programming course within Hudiksvalls municipal adult education (kommunal vuxenutbildning), run in collaboration with social services (socialtjänsten). It exists to give people who have fallen outside the typical education-to-employment path a way back in — through programming, but more fundamentally through structure, community, and self-belief.

Our students are people who struggle — whether due to abuse, difficult upbringings, neurodivergence (autism, ADHD), social anxiety, or simply bad luck. They are not able to follow the conventional path of gymnasium → higher education → employment. We meet them where they are.

## Who We Serve

Three broad groups, all equally welcome:

1. **High-aptitude students** — Showing real programming skill and career potential. We actively work to connect them with IT companies for internships (praktik), where a workplace mentor guides them. National social services can subsidize salaries to incentivize companies to hire them despite lacking formal credentials.

2. **Interested learners** — Engaged with programming, expressing interest in pursuing it as a career. They may not be ready for internships yet but are building toward it.

3. **Stability seekers** — The largest group. They come primarily to get out of the house, build routines, and be part of something. They have a general interest in computers but may not program much or at all. Their progress is measured in showing up, not in code written.

## Central Principles

**No gates, no pace, no pressure.** Participation is entirely individual. There is no required curriculum, no minimum pace, no prerequisites. Some students build full projects; others never get past the basics; others don't code at all. All of this is acceptable.

**Meet people where they are.** A student who shows up consistently after months of isolation has made as meaningful a step as one who lands an internship. The platform and the course should serve both without making the first feel inadequate or the second feel held back.

**Empowerment over instruction.** This is not a traditional education — we are legally not allowed to issue diplomas or call it one, as it would compete with commercial education providers and conflict with students' financial support arrangements. What we offer is a space, guidance, and opportunity. Students create their own path.

**Results matter, but are not the only measure.** The course is expensive for the municipality. Politicians are most interested in group 1 — students who leave social services and become taxpayers. We need to show these outcomes. But optimizing solely for that group would betray the purpose: giving everyone a chance, not just those most likely to succeed.

**Accessibility is non-negotiable.** The course is free. The tools we use (freeCodeCamp, VS Code) are free. The platform should never assume prior knowledge or create barriers to entry.

## Design Implications

- Features for advanced students (AI exercises, projects, portfolio) should exist but never be the first thing a new student encounters
- The default experience should feel welcoming and low-pressure, not like a bootcamp
- Progress tracking should celebrate consistency (attendance, showing up) as much as skill milestones
- Coach/teacher tools should support the "check in on everyone" workflow, not just the "track top performers" workflow
- When facing a trade-off, ask: does this help group 1 without alienating group 3?
